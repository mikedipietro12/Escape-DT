Commercial Drive map — curved paths (archived experiment) Superseded by hybrid paths for the live app. Kept for comparison: backtracking steps left with U-turn curves; off-spine corners rounded.

Map style: Curved lanes (rejoins spine on north) · Hybrid (off-spine curves only) · Stacked lanes (every reversal steps left) · Spine crop · East Village

Standard (live app)

Straight segments and sharp 90° turns. Backward legs use gold stroke on the same spine column.

Curved lanes

Each southward reversal shifts the line 18px left with a U-turn curve. Off-spine detours round their corners.

What we're testing

  • Lane bump — each leg that heads south (y increases) steps the route line one lane left (18px west of the spine).
  • Turnaround curves — south reversals bulge upward and shift left; resuming north bulges downward and rejoins the main spine.
  • Same-direction travel — consecutive northbound or southbound stops share the same lane line.
  • Off-spine corners — Victoria, Venables, and Frances legs use quadratic curves at spine junctions instead of hard L-shapes.
  • Gold backward legs — still flagged when geographic walk is southbound; curved path draws on the offset lane.
  • Turnaround timing — U-turn and straight leg are separate strokes so the curve traces fully before the run continues (same pattern for south reversal and north resume).
  • Trace arrow — small arrowhead on the curved map leads the drawing tip (curved panel only).
